# Pizza Yolo Copywriting Guidelines

---

# Purpose

This document defines how Pizza Yolo communicates.

It is not only about writing text.

It is about creating a recognizable voice that feels as unique as the visual identity.

Every sentence should sound like it belongs to Pizza Yolo.

The brand should never rely on exaggerated marketing language.

Confidence comes from simplicity.

---

# Communication Philosophy

Write like a real person.

Not like a marketing agency.

Not like a restaurant.

Not like a corporate brand.

The tone should feel effortless.

Almost as if a confident friend is talking.

---

# Core Principle

Say less.

Mean more.

Whenever a sentence can be shortened without losing meaning,

shorten it.

---

# Brand Personality

Pizza Yolo sounds:

• Confident

• Friendly

• Relaxed

• Young

• Slightly playful

• Honest

• Straightforward

Never:

• Loud

• Arrogant

• Try-hard

• Overly American

• Overly Italian

• Corporate

• Cringe

---

# Writing Style

Short sentences.

Natural language.

Minimal punctuation.

No unnecessary adjectives.

Avoid explaining obvious things.

Trust the visuals.

The design does half of the storytelling.

---

# Tone Examples

Instead of

"Experience our premium handcrafted Detroit Style Pizza made with the finest ingredients."

Write

"Detroit Style Pizza.
Fresh every day."

---

Instead of

"Our mission is to provide unforgettable culinary experiences."

Write

"Good pizza.
Good people.
Good summers."

---

Instead of

"Crafted with passion."

Write nothing.

If the food is good, people will see it.

---

# What We Never Say

Never use words like:

Premium

Luxury

Artisanal

Authentic

Award-winning

Best in town

World class

Exceptional quality

Finest ingredients

Unique experience

Handcrafted with love

Traditional recipes

Secret recipe

Family recipe

Hidden gem

These phrases are overused.

The brand should earn those perceptions rather than claim them.

---

# Headline Philosophy

Headlines should feel like poster titles.

Usually between one and six words.

Examples:

Detroit by the Beach.

Fresh Like You.

Love at First Slice.

Meet You at Sunset.

Grab a Slice.

See You Soon.

One More Slice.

Worth the Drive.

No Plans.
Just Pizza.

Small Menu.
Big Mood.

---

# Supporting Copy

Supporting paragraphs should stay short.

Maximum three sentences.

Never create walls of text.

Example:

Detroit Style Pizza with crispy cheese edges, soft dough and fresh toppings.

Served daily at Club Aliga.

---

# Calls to Action

Buttons should feel direct.

Preferred examples:

Visit Us

View Menu

Find Us

Get Directions

See Events

Follow Us

See Gallery

Today's Menu

Avoid:

Learn More

Click Here

Read More

Discover More

Start Now

---

# Product Naming

Keep product names simple.

Easy to remember.

Easy to order.

Current Menu:

Trio

Pepperoni

Ham & Corn

Avoid long descriptions.

The product names should become recognizable over time.

---

# Product Descriptions

One sentence.

Maximum.

Example:

Pepperoni

Loaded with crispy pepperoni and melted mozzarella.

---

Trio

Three flavors.
One slice.

---

Ham & Corn

Simple.
Classic.
Comfort food.

Descriptions should create appetite.

Not explain recipes.

---

# Hero Copy

The hero should immediately answer:

What is this?

Where is it?

Example:

Detroit Style Pizza
By the Beach.

Fresh Detroit Style Pizza served daily at Club Aliga.

That's enough.

---

# About Section

Do not tell the company's history.

Tell people what they can expect.

Instead of:

Pizza Yolo was founded in...

Write:

Fast slices.

Cold drinks.

Summer afternoons.

Sunsets.

That's Pizza Yolo.

---

# Experience Copy

Talk about moments.

Not products.

Examples:

Meet friends.

Watch the sunset.

Grab another slice.

Stay a little longer.

---

# Event Copy

Keep events exciting but simple.

Instead of:

Join us for an unforgettable evening.

Write:

Sunset.

DJ.

Pizza.

See you there.

---

# Menu Copy

The menu should be extremely clean.

Name

↓

Photo

↓

Short description

↓

Price

Nothing else.

---

# Website Paragraph Length

Ideal:

10–30 words.

Maximum:

60 words.

If a paragraph exceeds that,

split it.

---

# SEO Copy

SEO copy should never sound like SEO copy.

Keywords should fit naturally.

Bad:

Detroit Pizza Balaton serving Detroit Pizza Balaton visitors.

Good:

Looking for Detroit Style Pizza at Lake Balaton?

You'll find fresh slices every day at Club Aliga.

Humans first.

Search engines second.

---

# Social Media Voice

Captions should feel conversational.

Never over-explain.

Examples:

Fresh out.

See you tomorrow.

—

Good weather.
Good pizza.

—

Worth leaving the beach for.

—

Today's lineup.

—

Grab one before they're gone.

---

# Humor

Humor is welcome.

But only when it feels natural.

The joke should never become more important than the brand.

Avoid memes that age quickly.

Build recurring brand humor instead.

---

# Emoji Usage

Website:

Almost never.

Instagram:

Occasionally.

TikTok:

When it feels natural.

Never overload copy with emojis.

---

# Language

Primary language:

English.

The website launches in English.

Hungarian is a future addition, shown automatically to visitors whose browser or search language is Hungarian.

Until that switch exists, write everything in English:

Slogans

Merchandise

Packaging

Campaigns

Short headlines

Website copy

The English should feel effortless.

Never forced.

When Hungarian is added later, it should carry the same voice.

Never a literal translation.

---

# Photography and Copy

If photography already communicates something,

don't repeat it.

Example:

A large sunset image does not need the text:

"Beautiful sunsets."

Trust the visuals.

---

# Editing Checklist

Before publishing any text, ask:

Can this be shorter?

Can this sound more natural?

Does it feel confident?

Does it sound like Pizza Yolo?

Would someone actually say this?

If the answer to any is no,

rewrite it.

---

# Final Principle

The best copy often disappears.

People shouldn't remember the words.

They should remember how the brand made them feel.

Every sentence should support the atmosphere.

Never compete with it.
